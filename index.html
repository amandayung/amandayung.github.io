Researcher · Developer · Maker


I'm a senior programmer/analyst at the Center for Visual Science at the University of Rochester, as well as a masters student in Human Computer Interaction at the Rochester Institute of Technology. My passion lies in understanding the impact technology has on us and exploring ways to use technology to improve our lives.

You'll find me involved in just about everything: research, design, development, analysis, and dissemination. I'm constantly striving to acquire new and relevant skills to keep up with the ever-changing landscape of our tech-driven world.

At work I develop research techniques through open source libraries. I also use services like Mechanical Turk to improve behavioral and developmental research, both for researchers and participants. On the side, I'm currently interested in tangible user interfaces, the Internet of Things, and personal fabrication interfaces for novice users.
